WildLife

Our national parks host millions of people a year, sustain countless species,
and safeguard some of the most breathtaking landscapes on the planet.
They preserve natural and cultural resources “for the enjoyment, education,
and inspiration of this and future generations.” Here's how to make the most
of your next national parks trip.

American Bison
The American bison is a symbol of the American West and is known for its massive size and shaggy coat.

Asiatic Elephant
Asiatic elephants are smaller than their African relatives and are known for their intelligence and social behavior.

Bald Eagle
The bald eagle is a bird of prey found in North America, recognized for its white head and tail.

Black Bear
Black bears are adaptable and can be found in forests, mountains, and even urban areas across North America.

Black Stork
The black stork is a migratory bird known for its striking black and white plumage and long legs.

Wild Boar
Wild boars are known for their tusks and are found in forests and grasslands across Europe and Asia.

Catfish
Catfish are known for their whisker-like barbels and are found in freshwater and saltwater environments.

Chameleon
Chameleons are famous for their ability to change color and their unique, independently moving eyes.

Cobra
Cobras are venomous snakes known for their hood and are found in Africa and Asia.

Crocodile
Crocodiles are large reptiles found in tropical regions, known for their powerful jaws and stealthy hunting.

Deer
Deer are graceful animals known for their antlers and are found in various habitats worldwide.

Dragonfly
Dragonflies are agile insects known for their large eyes and ability to hover in mid-air.

Eel
Eels are elongated fish known for their slippery bodies and are found in both freshwater and saltwater.

European Brown Bear
European brown bears are large mammals known for their strength and are found in forests across Europe.

Falcon
Falcons are birds of prey known for their speed and keen eyesight, often used in falconry.

Fireflies
Fireflies are bioluminescent insects known for their glowing abdomens, often seen in summer evenings.

Flamingo
Flamingos are wading birds known for their pink feathers and long legs, often found in shallow waters.

Garter Snake
Garter snakes are common non-venomous snakes found in North America, known for their distinctive stripes.

Golden Eagle
The golden eagle is a large bird of prey known for its powerful build and impressive hunting skills.

Gourami
Gouramis are freshwater fish known for their vibrant colors and labyrinth organ, allowing them to breathe air.

Gray Wolf
Gray wolves are social animals known for their pack behavior and are found in various habitats across the world.

Great Hornbill
The great hornbill is a large bird known for its distinctive casque and vibrant plumage.

Green Lizard
Green lizards are known for their bright green color and are often found in warm, sunny environments.

Grizzly Bear
Grizzly bears are large mammals known for their strength and are found in North America.

Honey Bee
Honey bees are essential pollinators known for their role in producing honey and beeswax.

Jackal
Jackals are opportunistic feeders known for their adaptability and are found in Africa and Asia.

Leopard Cat
Leopard cats are small wild cats known for their spotted coats and are found in Asia.

Komodo Dragon
Komodo dragons are the largest lizards in the world, known for their size and venomous bite.

Mahaseer
Mahaseer are freshwater fish known for their size and are popular among anglers in South Asia.

Monarch Butterfly
Monarch butterflies are known for their long migrations and striking orange and black wings.

Otter
Otters are playful aquatic mammals known for their agility in water and social behavior.

Owl
Owls are nocturnal birds of prey known for their distinctive calls and silent flight.

Indian Pangolin
Pangolins are unique mammals known for their protective keratin scales and are often called "scaly anteaters."

Parakeet
Parakeets are small, colorful birds known for their playful nature and ability to mimic sounds.

Peacock
Peacocks are known for their vibrant plumage and elaborate courtship displays.

Penguin
Penguins are flightless birds known for their distinctive tuxedo-like appearance and swimming ability.

Pheasant
Pheasants are colorful birds known for their long tails and are often found in grasslands and forests.

Python
Pythons are large non-venomous snakes known for their constricting method of hunting.

Rat Snake
Rat snakes are non-venomous constrictors known for their ability to control rodent populations.

Red Fox
Red foxes are known for their cunning nature and adaptability to various environments.

River Dolphin
River dolphins are freshwater dolphins known for their unique appearance and social behavior.

Royal Bengal Tiger
The Royal Bengal tiger is a majestic big cat known for its strength and beautiful striped coat.

Rubber Boa
Rubber boas are small, non-venomous snakes known for their smooth, rubbery skin.

Scarlet Minivet
The scarlet minivet is a small bird known for its striking red and black plumage.

Scorpion
Scorpions are arachnids known for their venomous sting and are found in various habitats worldwide.

Snow Leopard
Snow leopards are elusive big cats known for their beautiful spotted coats and adaptability to cold climates.

Snub-Nosed Monkey
Snub-nosed monkeys are known for their distinctive flat noses and are found in the mountains of Asia.

Tortoise
Tortoises are land-dwelling reptiles known for their hard shells and long lifespans.

Tragopan
Tragopans are colorful pheasants known for their elaborate courtship displays and beautiful plumage.

Tree Frog
Tree frogs are known for their ability to climb and are often found in tropical and temperate regions.

Turtle
Turtles are reptiles known for their protective shells and can be found in both land and aquatic environments.

Vulture
Vultures are scavenging birds known for their bald heads and role in the ecosystem as nature's cleanup crew.

Water Dragon
Water dragons are lizards known for their ability to swim and are often found near water sources.

Woodpecker
Woodpeckers are known for their pecking behavior and are often found in forests, creating nesting holes in trees.

Hippopotamus
Hippopotamuses are large, mostly herbivorous mammals known for their massive size and semi-aquatic lifestyle.

Rhinoceros
Rhinoceroses are large, thick-skinned herbivores known for their horns and are found in Africa and Asia.

Lion
Lions are known as the "king of the jungle" and are social animals that live in prides.

Zebra
Zebras are known for their distinctive black and white stripes and are social animals found in Africa.

Giraffe
Giraffes are the tallest land animals, known for their long necks and unique spotted coats.

Rabbit
Rabbits are small mammals known for their long ears and are often kept as pets or found in the wild.
